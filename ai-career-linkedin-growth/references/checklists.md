# Quality Checklists

## LinkedIn Profile Checklist

- Public search results for the user's name do not weaken the professional image.
- Headline contains target role and technical keywords.
- About section explains the user's project and proof.
- First 3 lines of About are strong enough before "see more".
- Experience sections include context, missions, stack, and results.
- Education includes school, diploma, dates, and useful description.
- Certifications are visible and ordered by relevance.
- Skills include both tech and AI keywords.
- Featured section contains projects, GitHub, CV, or posts.
- Recommendations are requested from relevant contacts.
- Profile is coherent with the CV.
- Photo is professional and the OpenToWork frame is used when useful.
- Banner is present when possible and uses `1584 x 396 px`.
- Target companies, groups, recruiters, HR, CTOs, and technical voices are followed for watch.

## CV Checklist

- Single target role is visible at the top.
- CV is ATS-safe: no complex tables, no heavy graphics, no unclear columns.
- Summary is specific, not generic.
- Technical stack is grouped.
- AI projects are explained with tools and purpose.
- Bullets use action verbs.
- Old experience is translated into transferable value.
- No unverified claims or inflated expertise.
- CV and LinkedIn use consistent dates and titles.

## Cover Letter Checklist

- Mentions the specific company and role.
- Connects the user's skills to the company's needs.
- Includes one or two proof examples.
- Stays under 400 words unless requested.
- Avoids generic phrases like "I am motivated and dynamic".
- Ends with a clear next step.

## LinkedIn Post Checklist

- Hook is clear in the first line.
- Post comes from a real project, lesson, or question.
- Technical terms are used naturally.
- There is one practical lesson.
- Tone sounds like the user, not a template.
- No exaggerated claims.
- No engagement bait.
- Soft call to action is optional and natural.

## Networking Checklist

- Connection notes are under 300 characters.
- Notes mention a real context: company, event, post, project, or shared topic.
- The user avoids mass-inviting many people from the same company.
- Comments add value in 3 to 5 lines instead of only saying thanks.
- Similar profiles have been benchmarked for keywords, certifications, and credible wording.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- "I am passionate about AI" without proof.
- Listing tools without showing use cases.
- Saying "expert" too early.
- Overusing AI-generated formatting.
- Publishing generic motivational posts.
- Asking recruiters for a job without context.
- Hiding availability or target contract.
- Making old experience dominate the new AI positioning.
- Sending generic connection requests without context.
