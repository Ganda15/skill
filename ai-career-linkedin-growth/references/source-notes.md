# Source Notes

Use these sources for grounding when updating this skill.

## Internal Workshop Notes

The user's trainer emphasized:

- LinkedIn is a professional identity showcase and extension of the CV.
- LinkedIn is also a search engine, networking tool, and information watch surface.
- Online image matters before LinkedIn: search the user's name and clean up weak public signals when needed.
- Recruiters scan profiles quickly for coherence, keywords, credibility, and proof.
- Recruiters use LinkedIn to verify background, understand the person, and recruit specific skills.
- Headlines must be clear and target-role oriented.
- The About section should reassure on both coding ability and AI ability.
- Experience sections need context, mission, responsibilities, detailed tasks, stack, and keywords.
- Education and certifications should be precise and ordered by relevance.
- Recommendations and skill endorsements build trust.
- Profile completeness improves visibility and recommendation potential.
- Network quality matters as much as volume; personalize connection notes and avoid mass-inviting many people from one company.
- Connection notes should stay short, ideally under LinkedIn's 300-character limit.
- LinkedIn activity can be minimal but should be targeted: follow, comment, connect, post.
- Useful exercises include benchmarking 3 to 5 target profiles for repeated keywords, finding one relevant target-company contact, and writing 3 to 5 line value-added comments.
- Visual guidance includes a professional photo, OpenToWork when active visibility is wanted, a banner at 1584 x 396 px, and Featured items such as CV, training flyer, posts, projects, and certificates.
